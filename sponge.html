Statement of Interest

I have a strong interest in visual, and fractal is one of the cool geometry patterns that draws my attention. Combining my knowledge and skills in combinatorics, programming, 3D modeling and 3D printing, I made this slidedeck to demonstrate a step-by-step process of how I modeled level 0, 1, 2 & 3 Menger's Sponge and 3D printed them as a cool-looking decorative object.

Overview

  1. Understand Fractal and Recursion
  2. 3D Model Fractal Objects using CAD
  3. Setup 3D printer and print it
  4. (Optional) Distribute design

Menger Sponge

- a 3D fractal of cubes!

"In mathematics, the Menger sponge is a fractal curve. It is a three-dimensional generalization of the one-dimensional Cantor set and two-dimensional Sierpinski carpet. It was first described by Karl Menger in 1926, in his studies of the concept of topological dimension." Read more about Menger sponge
